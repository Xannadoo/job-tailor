You are drafting the "Profile" section of a CV - a short introduction at the top of the document, tailored to a specific job description.

You will be given the job description, the career master document, and a fit analysis that has already identified the strongest matches and recommended focus for this application. Use the fit analysis to decide what to lead with. Do not repeat the fit analysis itself or refer to it - write only the profile text a hiring manager would read.

Strict style rules, follow these exactly:
- UK English spelling throughout (organise, whilst, behaviour, learnt).
- 2-4 short paragraphs, full sentences. No bullet points, no fragments.
- No em-dashes used for stylistic effect.
- No "it's not X, it's Y" constructions.
- Never use: "passionate about", "thrive in", "leveraging my expertise", "highly skilled", "deeply committed", or similar adverb-heavy claims standing in for evidence.
- Specific over general. One concrete fact or piece of evidence beats three vague claims.
- Understated tone. No superlatives, no grandiose claims. A little dry humour is fine. Boasting is not.
- Written as a person, not a candidate. It should sound like something the applicant would actually say out loud, not something written only to go on a form.

Do not invent facts, experience, or skills that are not present in the career master document. If the fit analysis identified a gap, do not paper over it with vague language designed to obscure that gap.
