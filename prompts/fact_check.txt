3. Classify it as one of:

   - UNSUPPORTED: not present in the master document at all.    - INCORRECT: contradicts the master document (e.g. wrong dates, wrong institution, wrong outcome).
   - HONEST DISCLOSURE: the claim states that the applicant does NOT have a particular skill, tool, or experience. This is not a fabrication and should never be marked UNSUPPORTED or INCORRECT, and should never be removed or corrected away. An honest gap disclosure is a feature of the draft, not a bug - flagging or deleting it would make the draft less truthful, not more.

Read carefully for negation before classifying. "I have experience with X" and "despite not having experience with X" are opposite claims. Check which one is actually written, word for word, before deciding whether something is a fabrication. A claim that explicitly states an absence of a skill can never be an UNSUPPORTED claim of having that skill - re-read the sentence if you find yourself about to flag a negated claim as unsupported, since this most likely means the negation was misread.

Be strict on actual fabrications. A vague skill category being true ("has used machine learning techniques") does not justify a specific unsupported claim ("has used XGBoost and random forests") if the specific tool is not named anywhere in the master document. Related is not the same as stated.

Watch for a different error shape: a narrow, true fact being used to license a stronger or broader claim sitting on top of it. A sentence can contain one fragment that is technically true and another part that overreaches beyond it - check each part, not just the easiest one to verify. For example, "fluent in Danish, having passed PD3" contains a true fact (passed PD3) and a separate, stronger claim (fluent) - the exam being passed does not make the fluency claim true, and the two must be checked independently. If the master document itself describes a weaker or different version of the same thing (e.g. "conversational Danish," "still sound mærkeligt," a skill described as basic, occasional, or limited), a draft claiming the stronger version is INCORRECT, even though it is built around a real fact. Identify the single strongest word or claim in the sentence (the adjective or capability being asserted, not just the easily-checkable fact attached to it) and check that specific word against the source, rather than letting a true fragment carry an unsupported escalation along with it.

Separately, recognise that not every claim needs the same kind of evidence. Technical or factual claims (tools, techniques, qualifications, employers, dates) need a traceable basis in the master document - either stated directly, or clearly covered by something that is stated (e.g. a specific technique covered in a named course, or a project that demonstrably required a named skill even if the skill itself isn't named verbatim). These fail by having no basis at all, direct or indirect - flag them as UNSUPPORTED in that case. Character or working-style claims (e.g. "demonstrates strong work ethic," "shows maturity and responsibility," "works well under pressure") are different: the master document will rarely state these in so many words, so the right test is not "is this written down" but "is this a reasonable reading of a real pattern in the document" - e.g. running a project or team unsupervised, sustained roles requiring trust and accuracy, advocating for others under pressure. Mark this kind of claim SUPPORTED if a real pattern in the document would reasonably lead a fair reader to it, and only flag it as UNSUPPORTED if it is not grounded in any actual pattern, not merely because no single sentence states it outright.

Pay particular attention to dates and timelines - check graduation dates, employment dates, and any claim about current status (e.g. "recent graduate", "currently studying") against the actual dates in the master document and the fact that today's date may be well after those events. Do not assume the language used in a job description ("recent graduates") describes the applicant's actual current status unless the master document confirms it.
